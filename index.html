Northeastern Electric Racing Drivetrain


Designed test pieces for our differential case incorporating complex geometry to test the robustness and effectiveness of different coatings to ensure proper sealing of our differential.



×

Northeastern Electric Racing Drivetrain

Section View of the Differential Case.

Problem
I was presented with the task of finding the optimal sealant/sealing method for a liquid lubricated GMC Yukon differential while utilizing a previously designed and made 3D-printed differential case.

Action
I designed and printed 3 unique test pieces that utilized the same material and print properties of the differential case. The test pieces were designed to contain some of the various internal geometries that the real case contained to investigate the effectiveness of different coatings (plastidip, a 2 part epoxy and a non-name brand spray coating were tested). A reinforcement plate was also designed and laser cut for the access panel on the bottom of the case in order to help distribute the pressure required to compress the gasket.

Result
These efforts led to us learning a lot about the various sealing techniques from gaskets to the various sealants. Ultimately, the case was able to retain the lubricant and no liquids were spilled at the subsequent competition during our tilt test where the car is tilted at 45 and 60 degrees. This aided in the car passing its first ever technical inspection and coming in 2nd in the EV category at FHE.

Northeastern Electric Racing Project

Photo of the rear corner assembly

Problem
Create a FSAE/FHE rules compliant suspension and steering systems for a formula style electric racecar to compete at our clubs first FSAE competition in 1 academic year. A goal of the project was to establish a platform for development for future NER generations in order to aid in the further growth of the club.

Action
I created and managed timelines for both the steering and suspension projects. I also made and upkept various sub-tasks to assign to a group of 5 other student engineers, managing them and ensuring that all blockers were addressed. I also created the geomtries for both the steering and suspension systems in SolidWorks utilizing a custom built dynamic calculator. I modeled 20+ unique components for both the steering and suspension system. I conducted simulations on these components to ensure that they passed various strength requirements utilizing FEA and other validation techniques. I also did some part optimization to ensure efficient material use so that components were as lightweight as possible.

Result
The suspension and steering are 30% of the previous iterations weight, improving performance drastically. The car is also predicted to handle signficantly better causing for less tyre degradation and faster overall lap times by roughly 15%. The platform created is also very adjustable and outfitted with many sensors so can be utilized as a testing device in the future for better and faster iteration. Our club has yet to compete so we are not sure how these improvements will fare at competition but we are very optimistic in our work and excited for the fututre iterations.
